Gemini history and notable controversy

By Ethan Stonecipher | January 30, 2026

Gemini: The Rise, Rebranding, and Resilience of Google’s AI Flagship

Since its inception, the AI model known as Gemini has stood as Google’s most ambitious answer to the rapidly evolving landscape of generative artificial intelligence. Developed as a unified effort between Google Brain and DeepMind (now merged into Google DeepMind), Gemini represents a nearly decade-long culmination of research in neural networks and reinforcement learning.

A Multimodal Beginning

Gemini was first officially announced at the Google I/O keynote on May 10, 2023. Unlike its predecessor, Bard—which was primarily text-based—Gemini was engineered from the ground up to be natively multimodal. This allowed the model to process and reason across text, images, audio, video, and code simultaneously.

The official rollout of the first Gemini-branded models began in December 2023 with Gemini 1.0 Pro, followed by the more powerful Ultra 1.0.

The Great Rebranding: From Bard to Gemini

For nearly a year, Google maintained two distinct AI identities: the Bard chatbot and the Gemini underlying models. This changed in February 2024, when Google consolidated its AI efforts under a single brand.

  • Bard was officially renamed to Gemini.
  • Duet AI in Google Workspace was integrated into the Gemini ecosystem.
  • A dedicated Gemini app was launched for Android, effectively beginning the shift from the traditional Google Assistant to an AI-first assistant.

The face of Google's flagship multimodal AI.

Notable Updates & Evolution

  • Gemini 1.5 Pro (February 2024): Introduced a breakthrough 1-million-token context window, allowing the model to process massive amounts of data, such as hour-long videos or thousands of lines of code, in a single prompt.
  • Gemini 2.0 (2025): Pushed the boundaries of agentic capabilities, focusing on reasoning and the ability for AI to perform multi-step tasks autonomously.
  • Gemini 3 (November 2025): The current flagship family, featuring Gemini 3 Pro and Gemini 3 Flash. This generation introduced "PhD-level reasoning" and significantly improved the model's ability to grasp context and intent.
  • Deep Research (December 2025): An autonomous agent capable of planning and synthesizing results for complex research tasks.

Controversies and Challenges

Despite its technical achievements, Gemini's history has been marked by high-profile setbacks:

  • The Launch Faux Pas: During the initial Bard demo in February 2023, the AI provided a factual error regarding the James Webb Space Telescope, which contributed to a temporary $100 billion slide in Alphabet's market value.
  • Historical Accuracy (February 2024): Shortly after the launch of its image generator, Gemini faced intense backlash for generating historically inaccurate images. Users found that in an attempt to ensure diversity, the model depicted racially diverse individuals in inaccurate contexts, such as Nazi-era German soldiers and America's Founding Fathers. Google paused the ability to generate images of people for several months to rectify the bias and accuracy issues.
  • "Fake" Demo Allegations: A promotional video showing Gemini interacting in real-time was later revealed to have been edited—the model was actually responding to text prompts and screenshots rather than live video and voice.

Current Status

As of early 2026, Gemini has been integrated into nearly every corner of the Google ecosystem, from Chrome to Gmail and Search. With over 650 million monthly users, it remains a central pillar in the global AI competition.
